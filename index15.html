Biryani

Ingredients

  • For Rice: Basmati rice, water, bay leaf, cloves, green cardamoms, black cardamom, cinnamon stick, salt, oil or ghee.
  • For Biryani Masala: Cumin seeds, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, red chili powder, black pepper powder.
  • For Chicken/Meat Marinade: Chicken/mutton, yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, salt, turmeric, red chili powder, garam masala, lemon juice.
  • For Biryani Preparation: Ghee or oil, onions, tomatoes, green chilies, coriander leaves, mint leaves, milk with saffron, kewra or rose water (optional).

Process

Soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then boil it with whole spices until 80% cooked, drain, and set aside. Marinate the chicken or mutton with yogurt, spices, and lemon juice for at least 30 minutes. In a pan, heat oil or ghee, fry sliced onions until golden brown, add the marinated meat, and cook until tender. Add chopped tomatoes, green chilies, and some coriander and mint leaves, cooking until tomatoes soften. In a heavy-bottomed pot, layer cooked meat at the bottom, followed by a layer of rice, then sprinkle fried onions, coriander, mint, and saffron milk. Repeat the layers and drizzle kewra or rose water for added aroma. Cover the pot with a tight lid and cook on low heat for 20-25 minutes (dum cooking). Let it rest for 10 minutes before serving. Fluff gently and enjoy with raita or salad!

Nihari

Ingredients

  • For the meat and gravy, you will need 500g of beef or mutton (preferably shank), 4 tablespoons of oil or ghee, 2 sliced onions, 1 tablespoon of ginger-garlic paste, 1 cup of yogurt, 1 tablespoon of wheat flour for thickening, and 6 cups of water.
  • For the spices, use 1 tablespoon of red chili powder, 1 tablespoon of coriander powder, 1 teaspoon of turmeric powder, 1 tablespoon of garam masala, 1 teaspoon of cumin powder, 1 tablespoon of salt (adjust to taste), 2-3 cloves, 2-3 green cardamoms, 1 cinnamon stick, and 1 bay leaf.
  • For garnishing, have some fresh chopped coriander leaves, julienned ginger, lemon wedges, and chopped green chilies.

Process

Heat oil/ghee in a heavy-bottomed pot, add sliced onions, and saut√© until golden brown. Add ginger-garlic paste and cook for a minute. Add beef/mutton and fry until slightly browned. Mix in yogurt and all spices, stirring well. Pour in water, cover, and let it simmer on low heat for 4-5 hours (or use a pressure cooker for faster cooking). Dissolve wheat flour in some water, add it to the gravy, and let it cook for another 15-20 minutes until the curry thickens. Garnish with coriander, ginger, green chilies, and serve hot with naan or paratha.
